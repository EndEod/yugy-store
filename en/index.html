Principle

Contractors are strong at ‚Äúhow to build.‚Äù Architects are strong at ‚Äúwhat‚Äôs allowed and what‚Äôs feasible.‚Äù A successful retrofit needs both, but risk control starts with feasibility and sequence.

Why an architect-led strategy?

Retrofits fail when feasibility, constraints, and sequence are not defined upfront. We integrate code, structure, building physics, and asset logic into one plan.

Scope of service

Not a generic report. A strategic playbook for retrofit execution.

1) On-site Health Check

  • Envelope condition, windows, insulation aging
  • Deterioration tracing: leaks, condensation, cracks
  • Baseline review: plumbing/electrical/systems

2) Code & Feasibility Review

  • Key: legal feasibility for additions / major renovations
  • Impact analysis under current regulations
  • Reuse potential and layout efficiency review

3) Retrofit Optimization Strategy

  • Prioritized upgrade roadmap (high ROI first)
  • Material/method recommendations for performance & cost
  • High-level performance improvement outline

4) Final Strategy Report (PDF)

  • Existing-condition base drawings (if absent)
  • Findings summary + risk register
  • Roadmap + recommended schedule and next actions
